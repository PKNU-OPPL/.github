## Overview

The Ocean Physics and Prediction Laboratory (OPPL) conducts research on oceanic and climate variability through the combined use of physical theory, numerical simulation, observational analysis, and machine learning.

Our work aims to improve scientific understanding of ocean processes and to develop robust methodologies for prediction, reanalysis, and environmental applications.

---

## Research Themes

- Physical oceanography
- Ocean circulation and air–sea interaction
- Ocean and climate variability
- Numerical ocean modeling
- Ocean data assimilation
- AI and machine learning for ocean prediction
- Marine environmental change

## Research Approach

Our laboratory integrates four complementary components:

- **Observations** for diagnosing oceanic processes and variability
- **Numerical models** for simulating dynamics and testing physical hypotheses
- **Data assimilation** for combining observations and models in a consistent framework
- **Artificial intelligence** for improving predictive skill and identifying complex patterns in data
